# Theme worlds — evaluation guide

One entry per theme, in unlock-ladder order. Every non-classic theme now has: a
full sound voice (reveal / flag / cascade / win), and most have board ambiance,
motion choreography, and a contrasting grout between cleared tiles. For each
theme: the world it is supposed to be, what shipped this round, and what to
check while playing.

**How to evaluate any theme in 2 minutes:** clear a few cells (reveal sound +
motion), flag a mine (flag sound + motion), open a zero region (cascade sound),
finish or abandon (win fanfare), and look at the gaps between cleared tiles
(the grout should clearly contrast the tile faces, never a merged slab). The
ambient particles should be visible but calm, and should make sense IN THE
WORLD — that is the editorial lesson below.

**Update status:** all 25 non-classic themes got sound voices. 24 of 26 have
board ambiance (classic and dark stay bare on purpose). Motion choreography was
deepened for dark, matrix, galaxy, sakura (reveal + flag) and every theme that
had set flag/mine timing tokens now actually animates with them (the tokens
were dead until this round). Aurora's title shimmer was fixed (a keyframes name
collision had it flicker-looping). The six quiet concept worlds gained their
first ambient effects.

## The deepening pass (2026-06-11): holistic fog + drawn objects

Two new layers, on top of everything above. **Evaluate these on a fresh board
(all fog) and again after clearing a region.**

**Holistic fog art.** Seven worlds now carry ONE drawing across the whole
unrevealed board — the fog tiles went translucent so the drawing reads through
them as a single image, and revealing breaks it tile by tile (you unfold the
paper, push past the charted edge, wipe the slate). Not per-cell decoration;
stand back from a fresh board and the picture should be obvious:

- **Origami** — the crane's crease pattern (bird base): diagonals, medians,
  the inner diamond, neck/tail kites. Valley folds are dashed red, mountains
  solid, the way real crease diagrams are drawn. You should feel "I'm about
  to fold this."
- **Cartography** — one worn chart: a coastline winding across many cells, a
  contour island, the compass rose in the top-right, a dashed route to an X,
  foxing stains, two old fold lines.
- **Comic** (rebuilt — the old buff tiles read as nothing) — a real inked
  page: heavy panel gutters dividing the board into multi-cell panels,
  visible halftone screen, speed lines, a yellow action burst in a corner.
- **Blueprint** — one cyanotype sheet: double border frame, a drafted part
  with dash-dot centerlines, dimension arrows, hatched detail, title block.
  Numbers moved to white line work (faint hue tints keep counting legible) —
  blue, black, and white, the famous look.
- **Chalkboard** — half-erased workings span the slate (circled figure, sums,
  a long-division bracket, an eraser smear), and the digits now bleed colored
  chalk dust instead of reading as print.
- **Noir** — venetian-blind light raking the whole board at a tilt: one beam
  through the office blinds.
- **Ocean** — a sounding chart: a minesweeper vessel on the surface line,
  sweep cable trailing, nested depth contours, sounding marks. The literal
  heritage world.

**Drawn object sets (sprite batch 1).** Ten worlds' mines/flags/strikes are
now drawn in their material, replacing the emoji: editorial (ink blot / red
editor's circle / blot burst with scattered type), sumi-e (wet ink circle /
brushstroke with cinnabar hanko / feathered bleed), blueprint (drafted mine in
plan view / red revision triangle / red-scribbled rejection), cartography (sea
serpent / expedition pennant / shipwreck), chalkboard (chalk-sketched bomb /
chalk pennant / scrubbed-out burst), splitflap (cancelled flap module /
luggage tag / module shattering mid-flip), galaxy (black hole / planted survey
probe / collapsing-star flash), circuitboard (a literal bug on the silicon /
probe clip / burnt-out chip), nest (speckled egg in a twig cup / feather /
folded omelette), and noir's losses now leave a **chalk body outline** at every
detonation — the crime scene accumulates. Undrawn themes keep their emoji.
(Emoji packs were cut 2026-06-12 with the Collection declutter; the theme
is the only object-emoji source now.)

## Free at level 0

**Classic** — The 90s original. Deliberately untouched: default synth voice,
no ambiance, stock motion. Its restraint IS the identity. Evaluate: it should
feel exactly like it always has.

**Dark** — Hushed night. No ambiance by design. NEW: the moonrise reveal (tile
surfaces from black with a soft cool halo that brightens past rest, then
settles) and a quiet flag set-down with no bounce. Voice: low soft sines,
falling flag tones, a gentle minor-lift win. Evaluate: does it feel calm and
nocturnal rather than just "grey classic"?

## Quiet print and paper (L5-35)

**Editorial (L5)** — A broadsheet newspaper: ink on warm newsprint, column
rules, editor's red. Voice: typewriter strikes, a carriage-return ding on the
flag, a news-bell win. Ambiance (REWORKED after your feedback — the falling
flecks read as dust and the sweeping rule as an airplane contrail): lines of
type now COMPOSE THEMSELVES on the page — thin ink rules fade in at rest and
fade away, with small ink specks blinking like setting type; nothing falls,
nothing flies. Grout: column-rule ink. Evaluate: does the board read as a page
being typeset?

**Sumi-e (L10)** — Japanese ink-brush on rice paper; one cinnabar seal-red
accent. Voice: soft slow triangles, a serene min7 wash on the win. Ambiance:
ink motes bloom and feather on the paper (rarely one in seal-red), long brush
wisps float through. Grout: sumi brush line. Evaluate: zen — the quietest
world; nothing should ever feel urgent.

**Blueprint (L15)** — Cyan drafting lines on navy blueprint paper, technical
monospace. Voice: precise triangle ticks, quartal-harmony win. Ambiance: small
cyan drafting crosses register like compass pricks; dashed measure lines sweep
the sheet. Grout: the pale drafting grid — cleared cells read as ruled panels.
Evaluate: does clearing feel like drawing up a plan?

**Cartography (L20)** — An antique treasure chart: sepia ink, parchment,
X-marks-the-spot. Voice: low warm sines, a buoy-bell toll on the flag, a wide
low-major win. Ambiance: dashed course segments drift across the chart; tiny
sounding rings surface and fade. Grout: sepia chart ruling. Evaluate: the
plotted-route dashes should feel navigational, not random scratches.

**Origami (L25)** — Folded craft paper, soft pastels, crease shadows on every
tile. Voice: crisp triangle crease-snaps, light D-major win. Ambiance: pastel
folded-paper triangles glide down with slow paper rotation. Grout: crease
shadow. Evaluate: the gliding triangles are the one "falling" effect among the
paper worlds — do they read as drifting paper rather than confetti?

**Chalkboard (L30)** — Chalk on slate. Voice: felt taps (the original showcase
palette). Ambiance: chalk dust. Grout: chalk line. Mostly pre-existing;
evaluate that it still hangs together next to the new worlds.

**Noir (L35)** — A black-and-white detective film: film grain, smoke, a
whisky-glass win. Voice: muted low falling triangles; the win is a jazz chord
that never quite resolves. Ambiance: dust motes + film grain (pre-existing).
Reveal: the projector flicker (pre-existing, judged already in-character).
Evaluate: monochrome mood — does it stay quiet and smoky?

## Gentle nature (L40-60)

**Ocean (L40)** — Soft deep water. Voice: slow low sines, an anchor-drop flag,
tiny rising bubble cascade. Ambiance: floating bubbles (pre-existing). Grout:
sea-glass blue. Evaluate: weight and slowness — it should feel submerged.

**Forest (L45)** — Woodland: falling leaves, fireflies. Voice: two-note bird
chirps (the one two-tone reveal), fluttering cascade, dawn-chorus win.
Evaluate: the chirps fire on every reveal — do they stay charming under fast
play?

**Sakura (L50)** — Cherry blossoms and lanterns at dusk. Voice: koto plucks on
the hirajoshi scale, falling to the root. NEW motion: the petal-landing reveal
(drifts in from up-left, overswings, rocks back once) and the lantern-hang
flag (drops in and sways once around its hook). Evaluate: the two-direction
rock is the signature — does it read as a petal landing?

**Apothecary (L55)** — A candle-lit potion shop: dark wood, glass vials.
Voice: high glass clinks, a sparkle-arpeggio win. Ambiance: candle glow +
motes (pre-existing). Evaluate: the clinks against the wood-dark palette.

**Split-Flap (L60)** — A Solari airport departure board. REDESIGNED this
round: sleeping near-black flaps vs live lit modules with a top-lip sheen and
center split, heavy backlit glyphs, steel rails. Voice: dry mechanical
double-ticks, a near-monotone flap shuffle cascade, and a DESCENDING PA
boarding chime on the win (the one descending win in the game). Evaluate: do
flipped cells now read as a real departure board? (Your earlier report drove
this.)

## Rich light (L65-80)

**Stained Glass (L65)** — A cathedral window: jewel panes in dark lead.
Voice: bell tones through glass (pre-existing palette). Ambiance: jewel-light
glow. Grout: dark lead came. Evaluate: the glassBloom reveal against the lead.

**Aurora (L70)** — Polar night sky. Voice: long glassy sines, a shimmering
wide-interval win. NEW: the reveal is a curtain of light passing over the tile
with a green-teal hue swing — and the TITLE's gradient shimmer is fixed (it
had been flicker-looping for weeks due to a keyframes name collision).
Evaluate: title shimmers smoothly; reveals sweep like light curtains.

**Galaxy (L75)** — Deep space: nebula drift, starfield. Voice: sub-bass
thrums; the flag is a star ping answered by the void. NEW motion: the
starbirth reveal (ignites from a point, flares blue-white past full size,
breathes back down) and the orbit-capture flag (swings in on a wide arc with a
magenta flash). Evaluate: the flare should feel cosmic, not like a UI pop.

**Candy (L80)** — A sweetshop: saturated pinks, sprinkles. Voice: a two-tone
bubble-POP reveal from the mid register, saccharine high-sparkle win.
Evaluate: bouncy without being shrill; flag timing now lands at its designed
snappiness (dead-token fix).

## Loud (L85-120)

**Nest (L85)** — A clutch under the open sky (replaced the retired comic theme
2026-06-25): bright sky-blue unrevealed cells with drifting clouds + a corner
sun, eggshell nest tiles below where the eggs (mines) hide. Voice: soft high
sine birdsong; a two-note chirp flag; an ascending dawn-chorus win. Ambiance: a
loose flock of slate gulls glides across the board, left and right. Objects: a
speckled egg in a twig cup (mine), a folded omelette (struck mine), a feather
(flag). Greg: a crab in a bird get-up — feathered wings, a beak, perched in a
twig nest. Grout: twig weave — cleared cells read as nest patches.

**Circuit Board (L90)** — Live PCB: copper traces, LEDs. Voice: logic-probe
blips (pre-existing). Ambiance: LED pulses. Grout: copper trace. Evaluate:
flag timing now lands (dead-token fix).

**Matrix (L95)** — The green terminal rain. Voice: high glyph ticks; the
cascade DESCENDS like the rain; the win blooms major out of the gloom. NEW
motion: the decode reveal (phosphor flash, two misregistered glitch frames,
afterglow decay, steps(6) easing — nothing in this world moves smoothly) and
the target-lock flag (snaps in oversized and hot, locks down in discrete
steps). Evaluate: the stepped, never-smooth feel.

**Neon (L100)** — A buzzing neon sign. Voice: a same-pitch double-sputter
reveal (a flickering tube), E-major flash win. Ambiance: CRT scan + sparks.
Evaluate: the sputter is the signature — does it read as electric?

**Synthwave (L105)** — 1980s retrowave: hot grid, magenta/cyan. Voice:
sawtooth Cm7 arps. Evaluate: distinct from Neon despite both being saw-based
(arpeggiated and musical vs sputtering and electric).

**Inferno (L110)** — Hellfire: lava cracks between charred tiles. Voice: low
sawtooth growls, a minor brass win. Ambiance: fire + rising sparks. Evaluate:
menace without muddiness on the low growls.

**Supernova (L115)** — A stellar blast. Voice: bright sawtooth zap reveal,
charge-and-flash flag, blazing octave win. Ambiance: burst rings + sparks.
Evaluate: should out-shout everything except Legendary.

**Legendary (L120)** — The gold-and-dragon capstone. Voice: herald sawtooth
brass in rising fifths, a coronation fanfare. Ambiance: gold sparkles +
embers. Grout: gold inlay. Evaluate: the reward feel — maximalism earned.
